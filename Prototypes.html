What is a Prototype?

A prototype is like a working wireframe. It should have most or all of the functionality of the following:

  • Wireframe
  • Annotations
  • Site structure (or sitemap)
  • Interactivity (at least navigation)

A good prototype should help you tell the story of how your proposed design would look and function.

Things to Avoid


  • Using actual content (this is a waste of time)
  • Writing unnecessary scripts or programs
  • Unnecessary fidelity
  • Getting attached

Tools for Building Prototypes

There are lots of different tools for prototyping--and some are free

LucidChart

Modeling, UML, ERD, Wireframes, Prototypes

Free (Premium Account if you register with a .edu email address)

Balsamiq

Simple low-fidelity prototypes

7 day free trial, $79 license

Invision

Intended to add interactivity to images. Create your screens in Photoshop or Illustrator, then import and add interactions.

Free and Collaborative.

Axure

Very Advanced Prototyping

Expensive, but free to students with transcript showing related coursework

Team Prototyping


Keep in mind that prototypes are intended for clients/users (primary and secondary) as well as programmers, designers, and information architects.


Some tools, like LucidChart and Invision, allow multiple team members to access files and edit simultaneously.


Axure allows sharing through Axshare hosting.
